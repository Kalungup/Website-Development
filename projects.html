Curiato - Smart Bed Monitoring Platform

As a project manager, I was fortunate enough to have the opportunity to work with Curiato, a digital health company that revolutionizes the way patients are monitored. Curiato's innovative smart bed monitoring platform has been designed to provide healthcare professionals with a new level of data insights that are used to inform clinical decision-making.

At the heart of the platform is the smart bed cover which collects sensor data and provides an accurate, real-time representation of the patient's condition. By leveraging the latest AI-based analytics, the platform is able to deliver actionable decision support, helping healthcare professionals to make informed and effective decisions.

One of the key applications of the Curiato platform is the pressure injury prevention, which addresses a critical and growing healthcare challenge. Pressure injuries are a silent epidemic that contributes to 60,000 deaths each year and result in longer hospital stays, higher readmissions and avoidable costs of up to $6 billion.

EVETTI Project

As the project manager for the Brighter Smiles organization, I am thrilled to be a part of the EVETTI project, an initiative aimed at strengthening the vocational education and training sector in Uganda. This project is a testament to our commitment to fostering positive change in the textile industry, both locally and globally.

Our team is dedicated to bridging the gap between education and technology by implementing a Hybrid Learning Strategy that combines eLearning with traditional educational methods. The curriculum we aim to renew will be updated with the latest skills that match the demands of the textile industry, while incorporating environmental and social governance into all aspects of the supply chain. This not only aligns with the global mandate of the textile design profession, but also helps to promote social entrepreneurship and innovation in the industry.

By working closely with our consortium partners, the Enhancing VET Textile Innovation (EVETTI), which consists of two universities with two scientific textile labs, we will be able to advance our knowledge in textile innovation and its role in the circular economy. The reference curriculum standards developed through the project will be nationalized and promoted by our project partner, NCDC, and shared internationally through various communication channels of each EVETTI partner.

The EVETTI project is a unique opportunity to contribute to the development of skills and competencies in the textile industry in Uganda, and to promote economic growth and expansion in the sector. We are confident that this project will help bridge the gap between education and the workplace by developing meaningful curricula for vocational training institutions. Overall, the EVETTI project is poised to be a major contributor to the sustainable development of the textile industry in Uganda and beyond.
